Founding Charter

SHINEX DAO FOUNDING CHARTER

Article 1 — Establishment

The ShineX DAO is hereby established as a community-owned treasury ecosystem governed by SX token holders.

Article 2 — Mission

The mission of ShineX DAO is to create a sustainable treasury-backed ecosystem that combines community ownership, structured governance, and professional capital allocation through approved copy-trading systems.

Article 3 — Treasury Ownership

The treasury shall be owned collectively by SX token holders.

No individual member, executive, committee, or strategy provider shall own treasury assets.

Article 4 — Governance Structure

ShineX DAO shall operate through:

  • ●Governance Layer
  • ●Executive Layer

The Governance Layer determines policy and strategic direction.

The Executive Layer implements approved decisions and oversees operational execution.

Article 5 — SX Token

SX is the native governance and ownership token of ShineX DAO.

SX grants:

  • ●Governance rights
  • ●Treasury participation rights
  • ●Ecosystem participation rights

Article 6 — Treasury Principle

Treasury capital shall remain under DAO-controlled custody at all times.

Strategy providers may generate signals but shall not have direct control over treasury assets.

Article 7 — Core Values

ShineX DAO shall operate according to:

  • ●Transparency
  • ●Accountability
  • ●Capital preservation
  • ●Community ownership
  • ●Long-term growth

Article 8 — Amendments

This Founding Charter may only be amended through governance-approved procedures defined by the DAO Constitution.

Constitution

SHINEX DAO CONSTITUTION

Article 1 — Purpose

ShineX DAO exists to build a community-owned treasury that allocates capital to approved copy-trading strategies while maintaining transparency, accountability, and long-term treasury growth.

Article 2 — Ownership

The ShineX DAO Treasury is collectively owned by SX token holders.

No individual, committee, strategy provider, or executive member owns DAO treasury assets.

Article 3 — Governance Authority

The Governance Layer represents the highest decision-making authority within the DAO.

Major treasury decisions must be approved through governance voting.

Each SX token represents one vote.

Article 4 — Executive Authority

The Executive Layer is responsible for implementing governance decisions and managing operational activities.

The Executive Layer may not override governance decisions.

Article 5 — Treasury Custody

All treasury funds remain under DAO-controlled custody.

Strategy providers shall never have direct access to treasury capital.

All trading activity shall occur through approved execution systems.

Article 6 — Treasury Protection

Protection of treasury capital is the highest operational priority of the DAO.

Risk management systems, reserve policies, and treasury safeguards must be maintained at all times.

Article 7 — Token Supply

The total supply of SX token shall be fixed at 1,000 tokens unless modified through a supermajority governance vote.

Any change to token supply must be publicly disclosed before implementation.

Article 8 — Transparency

The DAO shall maintain transparent reporting regarding:

  • ●Treasury value
  • ●Trading performance
  • ●Liquidity reserves
  • ●Governance decisions
  • ●Major operational activities

Article 9 — Sub-Communities

Both Governance and Executive Layers may establish sub-communities and committees to perform specialized functions.

Such groups remain accountable to the DAO and may be modified or dissolved through governance processes.

Article 10 — Long-Term Mission

The long-term objective of ShineX DAO is to build a sustainable, community-owned financial ecosystem where treasury growth, governance participation, and ecosystem development create value for all members.

1. ShineX DAO Overview

ShineX DAO is a community-owned ecosystem designed to allow members to collectively own, govern, and grow a treasury through structured forex trading activities.

By bringing together the resources of many members into a single treasury, the community can participate in larger opportunities that may be difficult to achieve through smaller individual accounts.

The ecosystem combines community ownership, treasury management, governance, and structured forex trading activities into a single coordinated framework designed for long-term growth.

Members participate through the SX token, which provides governance rights, treasury participation rights, and access to the ShineX ecosystem.

As the treasury grows larger, the profits may be reinvested into treasury growth, liquidity expansion, member distributions, and other DAO initiatives, creating alignment between community participation and the long-term success of the ecosystem.

2. DAO Governance Model and Sub-Communities

ShineX DAO operates through a two-layer governance framework designed to separate decision-making from execution.

This structure ensures that governance remains community-driven while execution remains efficient, accountable, and scalable.

Governance Layer (Decision Authority)

The Governance Layer represents the collective authority of SX token holders.

This layer is responsible for determining the strategic direction of the ecosystem.

Responsibilities

  • ●Approval and removal of strategy providers
  • ●Treasury allocation decisions
  • ●Risk framework approval
  • ●Liquidity policies
  • ●Profit distribution policies
  • ●Ecosystem expansion initiatives
  • ●Constitutional amendments

Each SX token represents one vote.

Governance determines what should be done but does not directly perform operational activities.

Governance Sub-Communities

The Governance Layer may establish specialized sub-communities to improve governance efficiency and expertise.

Examples include:

  • ●Proposal Sub-Community
  • ●Policy Sub-Community
  • ●Strategy Review Sub-Community
  • ●Risk Oversight Sub-Community
  • ●Ecosystem Development Sub-Community

These groups assist governance but remain accountable to the DAO.

Execution Layer (Operational Authority)

The Execution Layer is responsible for implementing governance-approved decisions and managing operational activities throughout the ecosystem.

The Execution Layer complements governance by transforming approved policies into practical execution.

Responsibilities

  • ●Treasury operations
  • ●Risk enforcement
  • ●Liquidity management
  • ●Copy-trading execution oversight
  • ●NAV tracking and reporting
  • ●Operational coordination
  • ●Governance implementation

Execution Sub-Communities

The Execution Layer may establish specialized operational groups, including:

  • ●Treasury Operations Sub-Community
  • ●Risk Management Sub-Community
  • ●Liquidity Management Sub-Community
  • ●Strategy Execution Sub-Community
  • ●Operations Coordination Sub-Community

These groups ensure effective implementation of governance decisions.
